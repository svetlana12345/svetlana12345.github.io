Homemade

There is no doubt that homemade ice-cream is the best, because you can tailor it to your own tastes. When I was a kid, one of our family friends would make homemade ice-cream for our birthdays with a hand-cranked ice-cream maker. We all thought it was fun to have a turn at the crank, but it got tiring fast. Now there's electric ice-cream makers, so there's no need for hand-cranking to make your own.

Favorite Recipe

This is an awesome, basic Vanilla Ice-Cream recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 2 cups half-and-half cream
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

Instructions

Combine all ingredients, stirring to dissolve sugar completely. Fill cylinder of ice cream maker (no more than two-thirds full) and freeze according to manufacturer's directions.

Serve immediately or store in the freezer in a covered container.

Local Favorites

Izzy's Ice-Cream

Izzy's Ice-Cream located on Marshal Avenue in St. Paul and 2nd Street in Minneapolis, Izzy's is hands-down my favorite Twin Cities ice-cream. They have over 150 flavors which rotate in and out through out the year, many using local and seasonal ingredients. Deciding on a favorite flavor is tough, but fortunately you get a sampler Izzy scoop with your order, so you can have two! Some of my favorites are Mexican Chocolate, Dark Chocolate Zin, Norwegian Chai, Salted Caramel & Sugar Cookie.

Grand Ole Creamery

The Grand Ole Creamery located on Grand Avenue in St. Paul and Cedar Avenue in Minneapolis, the Grand Ole Creamery has been a local favorite for more than 30 years. They make over 200 flavors of ice-cream with 31 up at any given time. Black Hills Gold and their Sweet Cream are two of my favorites.
